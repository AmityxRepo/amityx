## Verified findings [V]
- Brightwheel: #1 rated childcare software; ~$36–$1,800/mo, ~$200–350/mo at 30–50 kids; no public price table (sales-led "request pricing" flow). (mybrightwheel.com, bloomily.app, capterra.com)
- Procare: longest-established; deep financial/billing strength for complex programs; runs comparison marketing against Brightwheel. (procaresoftware.com)
- Lillio (ex-HiMama): smaller programs; educator-built; learning stories → portfolios → development reports; flexible pricing + free trial. (capterra.com, getapp.com)
- TeamSnap: category default for youth sports; Google Play complaints: freezing, black screens, "unskippable game ads" on free tier. (play.google.com, vantasports.ai)
- Spond: free core (events, RSVP, reminders, availability, payments); called "clear leader for grassroots youth sports in America" by one 2026 roundup; strong UK grassroots goodwill. (spond.com, vantasports.ai)
- Heja: easiest single-team app; clean UI, chat, availability, fee collection. (heja.io, vantasports.ai)
- Jackrabbit Class: 7,000+ youth activity programs (dance, swim, gym, cheer, music); enrollment/billing/scheduling/parent comms. (jackrabbitclass.com)
- Sawyer: class registration + payments + parent comms for kids' activity businesses; flat pricing across locations/students. (hisawyer.com)
- DanceStudio-Pro: dance-studio ops incl. recital ticketing, registration, online payments. (getapp.com)
- **Adjacent wave the founder's brief missed — AI family assistants (2024–26):**
  - Ohai.ai (founder: Sheila Lirio Marcelo of Care.com): parses emails/PDFs/photos/newsletters → events+tasks; Google/Apple/Outlook sync; Instacart automation; daily digest; premium from $9.99/mo. (ohai.ai)
  - Milo: SMS-first family copilot (GPT-4); ingests voice memos/screenshots/invites; daily SMS rundown. (usecarly.com roundup)
  - Skylight Assistant: text-message AI family assistant, no app required; proactive schedule mgmt; Calendar Plus $79/yr (rewards, meals, Sidekick AI). (assistant.myskylight.com)
  - Hearth Display: hardware wall calendar + $86.40/yr membership (Sidekick AI, chore rewards, meals) — priciest combo. (alphamothers.com)
  - Also active in roundups: Maple, Carly, familymind. Framing across all: "mental load"; one stat cited: families spend 30+ hrs/wk coordinating. (growmaple.com, familymind.ai)

## Implication for T-001 (the load-bearing conclusion)
The parent-side "AI inbox → family calendar" wedge is now CONTESTED (Ohai/Milo/Skylight ship it today),
but every entrant anchors on the household/to-do frame. NONE builds a child-centric longitudinal record
(timeline → development → memories) and none attacks the provider side. Differentiation must be the
child graph + provider network, not AI ingestion alone — ingestion is table stakes with a closing window.
